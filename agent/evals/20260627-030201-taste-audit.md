**Mode:** Curator / hungry demanding visitor. This is an AUDIT, not a verdict — it does not PASS/FAIL a sprint.
**Build:** `pnpm build` exited 0. Driven against production `pnpm start -p 3320`, Chromium headless.
**Console:** zero console errors on index, dish detail, and filtered views at both 1280x900 and 390x844.
**Data integrity:** grid `data-count` and rendered cards = 19 (matches `src/data/menu.json` count 19). Dessert filter returns 2 (Shrikhand, Monthal) — matches data. No horizontal overflow at 390px on any touched page.

## Ranked findings

### 1. Sticky filter bar lets scrolling content ghost through it — reads as a rendering bug
**Rating: blocks shippable**
The sticky category bar is `bg-paper/85` + `backdrop-blur-md`. At 85% opacity the blur is not strong enough to hide sharp dark content moving behind it. On scroll you can clearly read the previous card's text and see photo edges bleeding through the bar.
- Desktop: `20260627-030201-index-scrolled-desktop.png` (faint "Breakfast · Snack" at top edge) and `20260627-030201-index-bottom-desktop.png` (ghosted "Gujarati Undhiya / Aloo Poha / Pav Bhaji" and photo color behind the bar).
- Mobile is worse: `20260627-030201-index-scrolled-mobile.png` — "Gujarati Muthiya" name and the "₹100.00" price badge are plainly visible through the bar.
**Fix direction (verifiable):** scroll the index until the filter bar is pinned and confirm no card text/photo is legible behind it — bar must read as a solid/opaque warm surface (raise opacity toward 100% and/or add a bottom hairline + shadow). Verify at both 1280px and 390px.

### 2. Mobile filter row clips "Dinner/Lunch/Meal" with no scroll affordance
**Rating: blocks shippable**
At 390px the row shows `All  Breakfast  Snack  Dessert  Dinn…` hard-cut at the right edge. The scrollbar is intentionally hidden (`[&::-webkit-scrollbar]:hidden`), so nothing signals the row scrolls — the clipped "Dinn" reads as a clipping bug, and Dinner/Lunch/Meal look unreachable.
- Evidence: `20260627-030201-index-mobile.png`, `20260627-030201-index-scrolled-mobile.png`, `20260627-030201-filter-dessert-settled-mobile.png` (all show "Dinn" cut).
**Fix direction (verifiable):** at 390px the user must be able to discover and reach Dinner, Lunch, and Meal — add a right-edge fade/gradient or peek the next chip so the row visibly invites a horizontal swipe; confirm tapping through to "Meal" filters correctly.

### 3. Detail page lower half is empty — no story, no warmth payoff
**Rating: nice to have (high value for the "homely" goal)**
The dish detail is just hero + thumbnails + name + tag pills, then a large void of cream below (roughly the bottom 30–40% of a 1280x900 viewport, and a comparable gap on mobile). For a menu whose whole promise is "made the homely way," the detail page never delivers a sentence of warmth or description — it feels like a stub.
- Evidence: `20260627-030201-detail-khaman-desktop-full.png` and `20260627-030201-detail-khaman-mobile-full.png` (everything sits in the top half; the rest is blank).
**Fix direction (verifiable):** open any dish and confirm there is a short descriptive/warm line (or price/availability/"from the Navarathri stall" context) that fills the composition; the page should not bottom out in empty cream right under the tag pills.

### 4. Filtered grid leaves a dead empty column on desktop with few results
**Rating: nice to have**
With only 2 Dessert results the 3-column grid left-aligns the cards and leaves the entire right third empty, which reads as unbalanced/unfinished rather than curated.
- Evidence: `20260627-030201-filter-dessert-settled-desktop.png` (Shrikhand + Monthal, big void on the right). (Mobile is single-column and unaffected: `…-settled-mobile.png`.)
**Fix direction (verifiable):** apply a category with 1–2 results at 1280px and confirm the small result set reads as deliberate (e.g. centered, or a count/"2 desserts" line, or a tighter max-width) rather than top-left-anchored in a wide empty grid.

### 5. Photo crop/aspect inconsistency — some portrait shots show mostly background
**Rating: nice to have**
Cards force a landscape crop, so portrait home photos (Shrikhand, Khandvi, Laddo) surface a lot of tablecloth/lid/container rather than the dish. Shrikhand in particular reads as "container of pale stuff on a cloth."
- Evidence: `20260627-030201-index-desktop-full.png` (Shrikhand, Khandvi tiles), `20260627-030201-filter-dessert-settled-mobile.png` (Shrikhand showing wide cloth margins).
**Fix direction (verifiable):** scan the grid and confirm the dish is the clear subject of every card crop (no card where background dominates); consider smarter focal cropping for the portrait sources.

### 6. Hero eats the entire first mobile viewport before any dish appears
**Rating: nice to have**
Eyebrow + 2-line serif headline + 3-line tagline + filter row fill the whole 390x844 screen; the first card only peeks at the very bottom. A hungry visitor sees zero food above the fold.
- Evidence: `20260627-030201-index-mobile.png` (first card barely entering at the bottom).
**Fix direction (verifiable):** load `/` at 390px and confirm at least one full appetizing dish card (photo + name) is visible without scrolling — tighten hero vertical rhythm.

### 7. "Navarathri" tag casing/spelling differs between card and detail
**Rating: nice to have**
Card badge is uppercase `NAVARATHRI STALL`; detail pill is title-case `Navarathri Stall`. Minor, but it's the only special badge and the inconsistency is noticeable side by side. (Also note the source spelling "Navarathri" vs common "Navratri" — out of scope unless data is corrected.)
- Evidence: `20260627-030201-index-desktop-full.png` (uppercase) vs `20260627-030201-detail-khaman-desktop-full.png` (title-case).
**Fix direction (verifiable):** open a Navarathri-tagged dish from its card and confirm the badge label presents consistently between grid and detail.

## What's already good (keep)
- Serif/sans pairing is excellent: warm serif display headline + dish names, clean sans for tagline/labels/prices. Hierarchy is clear; price badge (terracotta on soft peach) is legible and tasteful. (`…-index-desktop.png`)
- Palette is cohesive and genuinely warm/appetizing — cream paper, terracotta accent, ivory cards with soft shadow. (`…-index-desktop.png`)
- Detail hero is large, crisp on 2x, with a tidy thumbnail strip and a clear terracotta active ring; "Back to the menu" is an obvious pill affordance. (`…-detail-khaman-desktop-full.png`)
- Entrance stagger and filter transition are intentional, not janky: a mid-animation frame showed cards arriving diagonally (`…-filter-dessert-desktop.png`) but the settled state is clean (`…-filter-dessert-settled-desktop.png`). Hover (shadow lift + image scale-105), focus-visible rings, and `prefers-reduced-motion`/`useReducedMotion` are all present in source.
- Mobile single-column cards are large, photo-forward, and a pleasure to scroll; tap targets are generous. (`…-index-mobile.png`, `…-detail-khaman-mobile-full.png`)

## Not verifiable this pass
- **Empty-state design:** every one of the 6 categories has ≥1 item, so no zero-result filter state is reachable through the UI. The empty/no-results presentation could not be exercised and was not judged.
